## Overview

This repository hosts the **published patent application** for an AI-based system designed to
automate the analysis of legal documents using **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**.

Legal texts such as judgments, statutes, case filings, and contracts are inherently complex,
context-sensitive, and highly unstructured. Manual review of such documents is time-consuming,
error-prone, and difficult to scale. This invention addresses these challenges by introducing
a unified NLP-driven framework that enables intelligent understanding, summarization, and
secure processing of legal documents.

The system is designed to support legal research, compliance analysis, and judicial workflows
by reducing manual effort while preserving legal context and accuracy.

---

## Technical Motivation

Existing legal document processing systems rely heavily on keyword search and rigid rule-based
pipelines. These approaches fail to capture:
- Contextual meaning across long legal texts
- Relationships between legal entities
- Sensitive information that requires anonymization
- The need for concise yet faithful summaries

The motivation behind this invention is to apply modern NLP techniques that can **learn semantic
representations of legal language**, enabling automation that is both accurate and context-aware.

---

## Core Technical Components

The patent describes an integrated system composed of the following key components:

### Named Entity Recognition (NER)
Automatically identifies and classifies legal entities such as:
- Case citations
- Statutory references
- Judicial authorities
- Petitioners, respondents, and advocates

This enables structured extraction of legally relevant information from unstructured text.

### Text Summarization
Generates concise summaries of lengthy legal documents while retaining critical legal meaning.
This supports faster case review, legal research, and decision-making.

### Data Anonymization
Detects and masks sensitive personal and legal information to ensure privacy and compliance,
making documents suitable for sharing, analysis, and storage.

### Unified Transformer-Based Architecture
The system is built around a shared transformer-based NLP backbone, enabling multiple tasks
(analysis, summarization, anonymization) to be performed efficiently within a single framework.

The invention is applicable to:
